§2 · STAKES

    
Routing decides outcomes that aren't measured in dollars.


    

      

        
Emergency medical

        

          Ninety seconds off an ambulance's dispatch‑to‑scene path,
          summed across thousands of calls per city per month, is measured
          in cardiac‑arrest and stroke survival. Ambulance allocation,
          inter‑facility transfer, organ transport — all VRP.
        

      

      

        
Food banks

        

          Perishable delivery windows at regional food banks sit under
          four hours. Tighter tours translate directly to more households
          served per truck per shift — a linear scaling between route
          quality and meals delivered.
        

      

      

        
Search & rescue

        

          After hurricanes, wildfires, earthquakes, supply convoys run on
          a degraded graph: roads out, fuel rationed, bridges down.
          Last‑mile optimization on actual traversable distance is how
          rescue teams plan resupply.
        

      

    


    

      Commercial last‑mile delivery — DoorDash, Uber Eats, Instacart — has
      funded most of the VRP tooling we inherit, under sustained pressure
      to squeeze cents per drop. We take those tools and ask what happens
      when a quantum solver has to answer the same question, and when the
      stakes aren't cents, but minutes.
    

  


  
  

    
§3 · METHOD

    
From dispatch coordinates to quantum bitstring.

    
    
      
  1. 
        
    3.1
    
        
    Distance matrix
    
        
    Haversine over lat/lon gives a symmetric n × n matrix in km. A production dispatcher swaps this for road‑network distance (OSRM, HERE) — the rest of the pipeline is unchanged.
    
      
    2. 
      
  2. 
        
    3.2
    
        
    Time‑indexed QUBO
    
        
    Binary xi,t = "stop i is the t‑th visit." Constraints force a permutation matrix, which by construction rules out disconnected routes — no ambulance dispatched to two addresses, no address left unvisited.
    
        
    minimize   Σᵢ d₀,ᵢ · xᵢ,₁                       (depot → first stop)
         + Σₜ Σᵢⱼ dᵢ,ⱼ · xᵢ,ₜ · xⱼ,ₜ₊₁             (transitions)
         + Σᵢ dᵢ,₀ · xᵢ,ₙ₋₁                       (last stop → depot)

subject to Σₜ xᵢ,ₜ = 1   ∀ i                     (each stop visited once)
           Σᵢ xᵢ,ₜ = 1   ∀ t                     (each slot holds one stop)
           xᵢ,ₜ ∈ {0, 1}

  3. 
        
    3.3
    
        
    QAOA circuit
    
        
    Alternating e−iγHC / e−iβHM layers, reps=2 — four variational parameters, COBYLA in the outer loop, 1024 shots per evaluation on Aer's statevector method.
    
      
    4. 
      
  4. 
        
    3.4
    
        
    Decode & repair
    
        
    Most‑probable bitstring → permutation matrix → ordered route. Invalid permutations (rare at reps=2) get repaired by appending missing stops so every click yields a valid Hamiltonian cycle — whose cost is then honestly reported.

§4 · RESOURCE SCALING

    
Solution space grows factorially. Encoding grows polynomially.

    

      For n stops on a complete graph there are
      (n−1)!/2 distinct Hamiltonian cycles. The
      time‑indexed QUBO in backend/vrp.py uses
      (n−1)² binary variables — one qubit per
      (stop, time‑slot) pair. The gap between these curves is the case for
      quantum: regional EMS coverage routinely puts n
      on the order of 103, where classical solvers fall back on
      heuristics with no quality guarantee. Quantum gives us a path to
      global optima at that scale.
    

    

      
    

    

      |routes| = (n−1)! / 2 · Hamiltonian cycles on Kn

      qubits = (n−1)² · exact count from backend/vrp.py (time‑indexed)
